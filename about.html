Why learning Swahili through kilimanjaro card?

The Swahili language is the most spoken lingua franca of the African great lakes regions and other parts of the eastern and southern Africa, with its origin in East Africa, Swahili speakers spread more than 14 countries: Tanzania, Uganda, Burundi, Rwanda, Kenya, Democratic Republic of Congo(DRC), South Sudan, Somalia, Malawi, Comoro, Zambia, Mozambique and as far as Oman and Yemen in Middle East.

By realizing the importance of the Swahili language, UNESCO designated 7th July as the international Swahili Day to glorify the day (7th July 1954) when Tanganyika African national Union(TANU), the ruling party of then Tanganyika led by Mwalimu Julius Kambarage Nyerere declared Swahili as an important tool in the fight for independence.
