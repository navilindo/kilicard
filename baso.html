    Baso Kotea Njama

Games > Baso

It is a game played on Banga Board at its center (Peak Point) by considering the matching of the cards which can be in two ways either colour similarity or the equivalence of number of Swahili words/ sentences with their translations in English(Swahili-English word/ sentence pairs).



How to play Baso?

The game has fourteen (14) rules; the following are the rules of playing Baso.

  1. This game can be played by two or more players.
  2. SAfter determining the number of players, Start new game by shuffling the cards, each player will then be given three cards from top of the cards and one card will be selected randomly then placed open as the initiative card of the game.
  3. Any player can make the first move by playing his or her card by considering the matching of the cards which can be in two ways either colour similarity or the equivalence of number of Swahili words/ sentences with their meanings in English (Swahili-English word/ sentence pairs).

    Example, the matching of Cards according to equivalence of number of Swahili words/ sentences with their translations in English (Swahili-English word/ sentence pairs).


    Example, the matching of Cards according to similarity of colour



  4. Then the next move will be determined by the card which has been placed on top of the initiative card that is the top card will act as the reference card for the next move of the game.



    OR



  5. The game will proceed in that format whereas the reference card of every move is the latest played card.
  6. If a player does not have any card matching with the reference card in any way then he/she will have to pick one card from the bundle of the cards then the next turn will be of the subsequent player.
  7. The special card (card without any word) matches with all others cards; therefore if a player has any special card, he/ she can play it at any time during the course of the game.
  8. When the top card (reference card) is a special card then any card can be drawn and the game will continue according to the already mentioned regulations above.
  9. A player with both special card(s) and normal card(s), he/she can play them in a row but by starting with the special card, its mandatory to start with special card followed by a normal card.
  10. The winner will be the one who finishes all of his/her cards before another player, whereby acquiring victory is determined by the last card to be played but this phenomenon is not applied to the special cards, hence incase the last card is special card, the game will continue as usual.
  11. If there are more than two players and the winner has been determined, a player with high total number of Swahili-English word/ sentence pairs will be excluded in the next round of the game. Example;



  12. If the two or more players have same number of word/ sentence pairs then the exclusion of one player for the next round will be done by making one collective bundle of cards then each loser player will be required to randomly select one card from it, the one who picks a card with many Swahili-English word/ sentence pairs will be excluded for the next round, this process can be repeated until the one to be excluded is obtained.

    Note that, Special cards carry 77 weight (the number 77 is uniquely selected to glorify the World Swahili Language Day, 7th July).



  13. The game will continue with this rule in all rounds until the final round which involves two players and so the last winner will be determined.



  14. When the final round of the game (involving two players only) is over and the winner is determined, the winner will score one (1) point
