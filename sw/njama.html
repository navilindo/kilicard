How to play Njama?

The game has twelve (12) rules, the following are the rules of playing NJAMA.

  1. The game is played by two players/teams whereby the peak of Mount Kilimanjaro is the boundary for each player.
  2. Start a new game by shuffling the cards then systematically distribute them throughout the board by placing them (frontside up) to each plain square in clockwise / anticlockwise direction.



    OR



  3. Every player should carefully look and examine the number and the values of the cards with the same colour lying at his/her side.



  4. If the side of one player has cards with higher value than the cards (with the same colour) at the opponent’s side, he/she can pick any card with the same colour from his/her opponent’s side and replace it with any other card from his/her side.
  5. The value of the card is determined by the number of Swahili ‐ English word / sentence pairs.
    Example, Consider the cards with red colour below.



  6. The special cards have the value of 77 weight(the number 77 is uniquely selected to glorify the World Swahili Language Day, 7th July).



  7. The special cards can also be used to pick a card from the opponent’s side but only once then it becomes normal card by losing its weight and power.
  8. The special cards also have the power to switch with any card without losing its weight but this phenomenon works only within the boundaries of one player.
  9. If both sides have cards with the same values on a specific colour of normal cards and special cards, then the replacement of cards with that specific colour from opponent’s side cannot be done.
  10. A player can move his/her card one step to the adjacent plain square in any direction.
  11. At the end of game, winner is the one who manages to bring together four cards with the same colour into four adjacent squares (at the peak of Mount Kilimanjaro) early before his/her opponent, if she/he is able to bring normal cards, she/he will score one(1) point.



  12. If the player manages to bring together four special cards into four adjacent squares (at the peak of Mount Kilimanjaro), He/She will score a super goal which is equal to seven (7) points.
